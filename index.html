I am a third-year Ph.D. student in mathematics at UC Berkeley working with David Nadler.

I received my A.B. in mathematics from Princeton University, where my senior thesis advisor was János Kollár.

My research interests lie in algebraic geometry, homological algebra and symplectic geometry. I am particularly interested in:
  • Geometric information contained in derived categories of coherent sheaves (e.g. Orlov's conjecture on the Rouqier dimension);
  • Birational geometry (the minimal model program, DK hypothesis);
  • Tensor triangular geometry;
  • The homological mirror symmetry and its application to studies of derived categories of coherent sheaves;
  • (Noncommutative) derived algebraic geometry.
