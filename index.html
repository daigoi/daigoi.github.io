I am a second-year Ph.D. student in mathematics at UC Berkeley working with David Nadler.

I received my A.B. in mathematics from Princeton University, where my senior thesis advisor was János Kollár.

My research interests lie in algebraic geometry, symplectic geometry and homological algebra. I am particularly interested in:
  • The homological mirror symmetry and its application to studies of derived categories of coherent sheaves,
  • Fukaya categories and constructible sheaves,
  • Geometric information contained in derived categories of coherent sheaves (e.g. Orlov's conjecture),
  • Birational geometry (the minimal model program, D-equivalence),
  • Derived algebraic geometry
